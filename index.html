Did You Know?

  • No Snakes - The legend says St. Patrick drove all the snakes out of Ireland, in reality snakes never lived there and it was actually symbolism for driving out paganism with christianity.
  • Leprechauns are Protected - In Carlingford, Leprechauns are offically recognized as a protected species.
  • The Harp Symbol - The national symbol of Ireland isn't the shamrock, its the harp. Even Guinness uses it just flipped upside down to avoid copyright.
  • First Country to Ban Smoking in Pubs - In 2004, Ireland became the first country in the world to ban smoking in all indoor workplaces including pubs.

Irish Food Menu

Irish Stew

The national comfort dish. Traditionally made with mutton or lamb along with onion, potato, and carrots.

Soda Bread

Traditional Irish bread baked without yeast, instead using baking soda. Every Irish family has their own recipes, often including raisins or seeds. Best served with warm butter.

Boxty

Classic potato dish from the North and West. Crispy on the outside, soft inside. Made from grated raw potato, mashed potato, flour, and buttermilk.

Seafood Chowder

Creamy soup filled with fresh seafood usually Salmon, cod, and shellfish. Served with brown bread to soak it up.

Cuddle

A Dublin speciality. What Irish families throw together from leftovers like sausages, bacon, onion, and sliced potatoes.

Full Irish Breakfast

The monster of breakfasts. Includes sausages, bacon, eggs, black pudding(blood sausages), white pudding, tomatoes, beans, and toast. Designed to fuel farmers through long workdays.

Jameson

Irish Whiskey. Lighter and less smokey than scotch.

Guinness Beer

The most iconic Irish beverage, brewed in Dublin since 1759. Famous for its creamy head and dark rich flavour. Tourists flock to Guinness storehouse to try it straight from the source.
